How to Find Low Competition Dropshipping Products

Low competition does not mean no one sells the product. It means there may be a specific angle, audience or offer that is not fully crowded yet.

Do not chase products with zero demand

A common mistake is assuming that no competition is automatically good. If nobody searches for the product and no marketplace has buyer activity, there may be no real demand. The better goal is to find products with some demand but less obvious saturation. These products often sit inside a niche problem, a specific audience or a use case that big general stores ignore.

Quvirl uses Amazon demand and Google Trends as filters so users do not confuse low competition with no market.

Where lower competition can appear

  1. Specific audiences: Pet owners, car owners, students, renters, gym beginners or new parents may need different product angles.
  2. Problem variations: A broad product can be reframed around a smaller problem, such as travel storage, desk organization or pet grooming cleanup.
  3. Bundles: Combining related accessories can make the offer feel different even when the base product exists elsewhere.
  4. Content angle: Better educational content, clearer FAQs or stronger demonstrations can create an advantage.

Use review count carefully

Amazon review count can help estimate visible competition. A product category with extremely high review depth may be difficult to enter without a strong angle. A product with moderate reviews, clear demand and weak competitor content may be more interesting. Review count should not be the only rule, but it can guide research.

Validate before testing

After finding a possible low competition product, check search trend, supplier cost, delivery time, images and pricing. Then search social platforms to see whether people already create content around the problem. A product with an underserved angle and enough demand can be more useful than a generic product that every dropshipping store already advertises.
